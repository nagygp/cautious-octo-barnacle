\begin{abstract}
\noindent
One reusable abstraction.\\[2pt]
$\bullet$\quad \texttt{apn\_comp\_additive\_bijective}:\;\;
if $f$ is APN and $\sigma$ is an \emph{additive bijection}, then $\sigma\circ f$ is APN.\\[6pt]
\noindent
The Frobenius twist is just the most useful instance. Affine pre/post-composition
(EA-equivalence) preserves APN for free, because affine maps are additive
bijections.
\end{abstract}

\bigskip

% =====================================================================
\section*{What APN means}

\[
\Gamma=\bigl(F:\mathrm{Type},\;[\mathrm{Field}\;F],\;[\mathrm{CharP}\;F\;2]\bigr)
\]
\[
\mathrm{IsAPN}(f)\;:\;\;
\forall a\neq 0,\;\forall x,y.\;\;
f(x{+}a){+}f(x)=f(y{+}a){+}f(y)\;\Rightarrow\; y=x \;\lor\; y=x{+}a .
\]
``Every nonzero direction $a$ has at most two collision partners.''

\bigskip

% =====================================================================
\section{The lemma}

\begin{lstlisting}
lemma apn_comp_additive_bijective {F : Type*} [Field F] [CharP F 2]
    {f : F → F} (hf : IsAPN f)
    {σ : F → F} (hσ_bij : Function.Bijective σ)
    (hσ_add : ∀ x y, σ (x + y) = σ x + σ y) :
    IsAPN (σ ∘ f) := by
  intro a ha x y hxy
  convert hf a ha x y _ using 1
  exact hσ_bij.injective (by aesop)
\end{lstlisting}

\bigskip
\subsection*{Why it is true (two properties, used once each)}

\noindent
Look at the collision for $\sigma\circ f$:
\[
\sigma(f(x{+}a))+\sigma(f(x)) \;=\; \sigma(f(y{+}a))+\sigma(f(y)).
\]
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep6pt
  \item \textbf{Additivity} pulls $\sigma$ out of each sum:
  \[
  \sigma\bigl(f(x{+}a)+f(x)\bigr) \;=\; \sigma\bigl(f(y{+}a)+f(y)\bigr).
  \]
  \item \textbf{Injectivity} (half of bijectivity) cancels $\sigma$:
  \[
  f(x{+}a)+f(x) \;=\; f(y{+}a)+f(y).
  \]
\end{enumerate}
That is a collision for $f$. Since $f$ is APN, $y=x$ or $y=x+a$. Done.

\[
\inferrule*[Right=\textsc{apn-transport}]
{
  \mathrm{IsAPN}\,f
  \\
  \underbrace{\sigma(u{+}v)=\sigma u+\sigma v}_{\text{additive}}
  \\
  \underbrace{\mathrm{Injective}\,\sigma}_{\text{from bijective}}
}
{\;\mathrm{IsAPN}\,(\sigma\circ f)\;}
\]

\note{The two side premises are \emph{exactly} the shapes of \texttt{frob\_add}
(additivity of $x\mapsto x^{2^j}$) and \texttt{frob\_bijective} (its bijectivity).
The lemma is the abstraction; Frobenius is the instance.}

\bigskip

% =====================================================================
\section{Instance 1: the Frobenius twist}

\noindent
Take $\sigma(x)=x^{2^j}$. It is additive (\texttt{frob\_add}) and bijective on a
finite field (\texttt{frob\_bijective}). So multiplying an APN exponent by $2^j$
keeps it APN:

\begin{lstlisting}
lemma apn_frob_twist {F : Type*} [Field F] [Fintype F] [CharP F 2]
    {d : ℕ} (hd : IsAPN (fun x : F => x ^ d)) (j : ℕ) :
    IsAPN (fun x : F => x ^ (d * 2 ^ j)) := by
  have heq : (fun x : F => x ^ (d * 2 ^ j)) =
      (fun x => x ^ (2 ^ j)) ∘ (fun x => x ^ d) := by
    ext x; simp [pow_mul]
  rw [heq]
  exact apn_comp_additive_bijective hd (frob_bijective_field j) (frob_additive j)
\end{lstlisting}

\note{The decisive line names the three ingredients in order:
\texttt{hd} (the APN input), \texttt{frob\_bijective\_field j} (the bijection),
\texttt{frob\_additive j} (the additivity). This is the engine that removes the
``$k$ odd'' restriction from Kasami: $x^{d_k}=(x^{d_{n-k}})^{2^{2k}}$.}

\bigskip

% =====================================================================
\section{Instance 2: EA-equivalence (for free)}

\noindent
An \textbf{affine} map $\sigma(x)=A(x)+c$ with $A$ additive and bijective is an
additive bijection up to the constant $c$ --- and in the collision both copies of
$c$ cancel in char $2$. So pre- and post-composing $f$ with affine bijections
preserves APN. This is \textbf{EA-equivalence}, and it costs nothing beyond the
one lemma above.

\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=large]
F \arrow[r, "{\text{affine}}", "\sim"'] & F \arrow[r, "f"] & F \arrow[r, "{\text{affine}}", "\sim"'] & F
\end{tikzcd}
\]
\begin{center}\small An APN $f$ flanked by affine bijections is still APN.\end{center}

\bigskip

\section*{One-screen summary}

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.6}
\begin{tabular}{l l}
\textbf{ingredient} & \textbf{role} \\
\hline
$\sigma$ additive & pull $\sigma$ out of the difference \\
$\sigma$ injective & cancel $\sigma$, recover $f$'s collision \\
$f$ APN & $\le 2$ partners \\
$\Rightarrow$ & $\sigma\circ f$ APN \\
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
