\begin{abstract}
\noindent
One short induction.\\[2pt]
$\bullet$\quad \texttt{truncTrace\_artin\_schreier}:\;\;
$L_k(x^2+x)=x^{2^k}+x$.\\[6pt]
\noindent
Here $L_k(u)=\sum_{i<k}u^{2^i}$ is the \emph{truncated trace}. The identity is the
bridge that rewrites the Kasami differential as a truncated trace --- the entry
point to the Dempwolff--M\"uller Theorem~3.2 engine.
\end{abstract}

\bigskip

% =====================================================================
\section*{Setting}

\[
\Gamma=\bigl(F:\mathrm{Type},\;[\mathrm{CommRing}\;F],\;[\mathrm{CharP}\;F\;2]\bigr)
\]
No finiteness needed --- only $1+1=0$. The truncated trace is
\[
L_k(u)\;=\;\sum_{i=0}^{k-1} u^{2^i}
\;=\; u + u^2 + u^4 + \dots + u^{2^{k-1}} .
\]

\bigskip

% =====================================================================
\section{The lemma}

\[
\forall k:\mathbb{N},\;\forall x:F.\qquad
L_k(x^2+x) \;=\; x^{2^k}+x
\]

\begin{lstlisting}
lemma truncTrace_artin_schreier {F : Type*} [CommRing F] [CharP F 2]
    (k : ŌäĢ) (x : F) :
    truncTrace k (x ^ 2 + x) = x ^ (2 ^ k) + x := by
  induction' k with k ih generalizing x <;>
    simp_all +decide [truncTrace, pow_succ, pow_mul]
  ┬Ę rw [ŌåÉ two_smul F x, CharTwo.two_eq_zero, zero_smul]
  ┬Ę rw [Finset.sum_range_succ, ih]
    rw [add_pow_char_pow, mul_pow]; ring
    simp +decide [show (2 : F) = 0 by exact CharP.cast_eq_zero F 2]
\end{lstlisting}

\bigskip
\subsection*{Why it telescopes}

\noindent
Two facts in char $2$:
\begin{itemize}\itemsep4pt
  \item \textbf{Additivity} (Freshman's Dream): $L_k$ is additive, since each
        $u\mapsto u^{2^i}$ is. So $L_k(x^2+x)=L_k(x^2)+L_k(x)$.
  \item \textbf{Square commutes with $L_k$}: $L_k(x^2)=L_k(x)^2$, because
        squaring is the Frobenius ring hom.
\end{itemize}
Write $T=L_k(x)$. Then
\[
L_k(x^2+x)=T^2+T .
\]
But $T^2+T$ is itself a telescoping sum:
\[
T^2 = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} x^{2^{i+1}} = \sum_{i=1}^{k} x^{2^i},
\qquad
T = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} x^{2^i},
\]
\[
T^2+T = \Bigl(\sum_{i=1}^{k} x^{2^i}\Bigr)+\Bigl(\sum_{i=0}^{k-1} x^{2^i}\Bigr)
= x^{2^k}+x,
\]
because every middle term $x^{2^i}$ ($1\le i\le k-1$) appears \emph{twice} and
$u+u=0$. Only the endpoints $x^{2^k}$ and $x^{2^0}=x$ survive.

\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=tiny, row sep=tiny]
T^2: & & x^{2} & x^{4} & \cdots & x^{2^{k-1}} & x^{2^k} \\
T:   & x & x^{2} & x^{4} & \cdots & x^{2^{k-1}} & \\
\arrow[from=1-3, to=2-3, dash] \arrow[from=1-4, to=2-4, dash]
\arrow[from=1-6, to=2-6, dash]
\end{tikzcd}
\]
\begin{center}\small Matched pairs cancel (char $2$); only $x^{2^k}$ (top right)
and $x$ (bottom left) remain.\end{center}

\note{This is the additive shadow of a geometric series. Over $\mathbb{R}$,
$\sum_{i<k}r^i=(r^k-1)/(r-1)$; here $r=$ ``square'', the denominator $r-1$ is
``square plus identity'' $=u\mapsto u^2+u$, and the identity says
$L_k\circ(u^2+u)$ recovers $u^{2^k}+u$, the numerator.}

\section{Where it lands: the Kasami differential as a trace}

\noindent
The Kasami collision analysis needs to recognise $x^{2^k}+x$ as
$L_k(\text{something})$. The lemma says that ``something'' is the
Artin--Schreier input $x^2+x$:
\[
x^{2^k}+x \;=\; L_k(x^2+x).
\]
This is exactly the rewrite used (twice) to turn a difference of $d$-th powers
into a truncated trace, feeding the Dempwolff--M\"uller decomposition
$\Phi(u)=L_k(u)^{q+1}/u^{q}$.

\begin{lstlisting}
    rw [truncTrace_artin_schreier, truncTrace_artin_schreier]
\end{lstlisting}
